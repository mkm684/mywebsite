UOttaHack

Ottawa, Ontario - Feb 2018

This was my first ever hackathon! Our idea for this event was to create an app where you could take a picture of a store receipt and a backend would then generate a form containing all the nutritional value of your groceries. I created the Android front end of the application to take a picture using the camera phone and send it to the backend. My team partner created a script which enhanced, binarized and processed a photo through an optical processing engine. Unfortunately, the UPC/FDA database we had scoped out for the project wasn't compatible with the proprietary item codes used by Costco (Our store of choice). Our team tried to pivot and create a mock demo database, but we wanted to have a realistic application with already available resources as the FDA DB. Unfortunately out trials were not successful. Lots of lessons were learned, and we had a good time as well!

SteelHacks

Pittsburgh, Pennsilivanya - Feb 2018
dri(C)ve
This hackathon took place at the University of Pittsburgh. My friend and I traveled a ten-hour drive from Ottawa to Pittsburgh. While there we created a software package to detect if a person is falling asleep. The idea was inspired by my father's loss of a co-worker who died while driving after falling asleep at the wheel. Falling asleep while driving is responsible for 72,000 crashes and 800 fatalities each year. Some possible applications of this project could be to help keep drivers, pilots and heavy equipment operators alert, help identify if someone is having a stroke.

StarterHacks

Waterloo, Ontario - March 2018
PILLar
This hackathon took place at the University of Waterloo. I teamed up with five students from Waterloo. The hackathon also included product design, marketing and business plan for the final demo. While there we created a hardware hack using Amazon Alexa, AWS and the dragon board provided by Qualcomm. The application is a pill dispensary for elder people, powered by Alexa. Users would ask Alexa for their pills, and it would communicate with the dragon board to dispense their pills. The team created a design for the product logo and a framework for the actual dispensary.

SF Hacks

SanFrancisco, California - March 2018
  Alexa How is at the door
This hackathon took place at the University of SanFrancisco State University. I flew over the continent with my friend at the weekend just for the hackathon. We designed an application that was powered by Amazon Alexa and AWS. The app would sync with your Facebook friends and use image recognition (OpenCV) of their pictures to tell you if any of them is at your home door. At the hackathon, we were able to train OpenCV to recognize faces from photos we took within the hackathon. However, the Facebook API section is currently under built in a post hackathon work to import data (images) for additional training of OpenCV
